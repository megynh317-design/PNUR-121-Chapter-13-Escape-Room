Room 1 — Admissions Office (Ch13 — Stroke) Unlock: APHASIA Left hemisphere stroke = expressive aphasia + right-sided weakness. Right hemisphere = poor awareness, impulsivity, difficulty recognizing faces. Never assume cognitive impairment from aphasia. Communicate: yes/no questions, slow speech, gestures, extra time.
Room 2 — Medication Room (Ch13 — HTN) Unlock: COMPLIANCE Normal BP on medication does not mean HTN resolved — it means the medication is working. Stopping lisinopril risks rebound HTN. Silent damage continues. Assess for nonproductive cough (ACE inhibitor side effect) and address with provider.
Room 3 — Ellie's Room — Night (Ch15 — Falls) Unlock: NOCTURIA Furosemide + nocturia = highest fall risk window is overnight. Bed alarm alone is insufficient. Scheduled q2h toileting, call light in functional right hand, bed at lowest position, clear path, adequate nighttime lighting. Right-sided weakness affects dominant side.
Room 4 — Bathroom (Ch15 — Incontinence) Unlock: FUNCTIONAL Bladder and urethra work normally — aphasia prevents calling for help, weakness slows mobility. Foley catheter is never first intervention (UTI risk). Habit training = caregiver takes her on schedule. Prompted voiding also appropriate.
Room 5 — Dining Room (Ch13/15 — Aspiration/Nutrition) Unlock: ASPIRATION Wet gurgling voice = aspiration. Stop all oral intake, notify RN, speech therapy swallowing eval before anything PO. Thin liquids harder than solids post-stroke. Albumin 2.8 = protein malnutrition = impaired healing. Dietitian consult needed.
Room 6 — Activity Room (Ch17 — Communication) Unlock: VALIDATION Redirecting dismisses emotional experience. Ellie is grieving her communication ability. Use humor to diffuse reluctance at start of relationship. Guided choices (not open questions) given aphasia. Face her directly, use gestures, written words, allow time. Never assume cognitive impairment.
Room 7 — Family Lounge (Ch17 — Depression/Suicide) Unlock: ASSESSMENT Hoarded medications = suicide risk red flag. Medication noncompliance in older adults can be intentional stockpiling. Must directly ask Ellie about thoughts of self-harm. Notify RN immediately. Remove medications. Depression in older adults often presents as functional loss, not sadness.
Room 8 — Hallway (Ch15 — Pressure Injuries) Unlock: SHEARING Massage of reddened area is contraindicated — compresses capillaries. Stage I = nonblanchable erythema, intact skin. Pulling up in bed = friction. Sliding with HOB >30° = shearing (sacral skin stays, subcutaneous vessels stretch/rupture). Diabetes + aging skin = physiological risk factors. Reposition q2h, lift sheet, HOB <30°, moisture barrier cream.
Room 9 — PT Gym (Ch15 — Iatrogenesis) Unlock: IATROGENESIS Missing PT begins the iatrogenic cascade: immobility → muscle weakness → increased fall risk → fear → more refusal → deconditioning. Iatrogenesis = disorder acquired from receiving or failing to receive health care. Minimum: walk to bathroom before/after meals and bedtime. Involve family. Modify PT plan.
Room 10 — Nursing Station (Ch13/17 — Sundowning/Delirium) Unlock: DELIRIUM Sedatives are contraindicated in older adults — depress CNS, cause confusion, increase fall/skin risk, not metabolized efficiently. Must distinguish sundowning (late afternoon/evening, known cognitive changes) from delirium (acute cause). Non-pharm first: consistent staff, familiar objects, lighting, assess for physical causes (dehydration, infection, pain).
Room 11 — Ellie's Room — Morning (Ch17 — Depression) Unlock: DEPRESSION Withdrawal, reduced appetite, "what's the point" = atypical depression in older adults. Does not present as sadness. Report to RN, document objective changes. If SSRI ordered, takes several weeks for therapeutic effect — teach family. Failure to thrive can develop rapidly if untreated.
Room 12 ⚡ — Discharge Planning (All Chapters) Unlock: RESOURCES Collision: patient autonomy + caregiver burden + SDOH barriers + clinical safety. Cannot dismiss daughter or Ellie. Family care conference with RN, social worker, PT, Ellie. Community resources: home health nursing, Meals on Wheels, county agency on aging, adult day programs, caregiver support. Document clinical recommendations regardless of decision made.
