Room 1 — Kitchen (Neurological / Stroke)
Unlock word: ASPIRATION
Wet voice + cough on thin liquid = aspiration. Small bites/upright does not address dysphagia. Stop oral intake, notify RN, speech therapy eval, NPO until assessed. Thin liquids harder than solids post-stroke.
Room 2 — Bedroom (Cardiovascular / CHF)
Unlock word: REPORT
3 lb overnight + ankle edema = CHF exacerbation signal — must be reported immediately. Not normalized or reassured. Assess edema, auscultate lungs, check weight protocol, contact provider.
Room 3 — Living Room (Neurological / Right Hemisphere Stroke)
Unlock word: ANOSOGNOSIA
Right hemisphere = cannot perceive own deficits. Patient genuinely believes he is fine. Fall precautions, assisted ambulation, family education that this is neurological.
Room 4 — Bathroom (Musculoskeletal / SDOH)
Unlock word: CAREGIVER
Mrs. H. is informal caregiver AND patient. Pushing through pain is harmful. OT referral, blister packs, home health nurse for medications, county agency on aging.
Room 5 — Dining Room (Metabolic / Type 2 Diabetes)
Unlock word: HYPOGLYCEMIA
BG 58 = hypoglycemia. First action for conscious patient: 15g fast-acting carbs, recheck in 15 min (15-15 rule). 911 not first action. Older adults: symptoms mimic normal aging.
Room 6 — Back Porch (Pulmonary / COPD)
Unlock word: VACCINATE
Cessation benefits occur at any age. Primary COPD priority = prevent infections. Influenza + pneumococcal vaccines are #1 intervention. Confirm current and obtain orders if not.
Room 7 — Hallway (Sensory / Neurological)
Unlock word: SAFETY
Documentation alone insufficient when patient nearly falls. Cataracts + glaucoma + left stroke weakness = compounding fall risk. Fall precautions, lighting, provider notification, eye exam follow-up.
Room 8 ⚡ — Front Door (SDOH Collision)
Unlock word: RESOURCES
Education cannot solve food access. SDOH = systems intervention: Meals on Wheels, county aging services, social worker, family care conference. Fluid/CHF tension: daily weights + provider clarification.
